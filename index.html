I'm an electronics engineering student at Kantipur Engineering College, still early enough in the degree that most of what I know I've learned by poking at things until they work.

Right now that mostly means C, circuits, and figuring out how the theory in my textbooks connects to anything real. I spent a summer as a research trainee at the Nepal Research and Collaboration Center, which turned into my first real piece of research — a review paper on how aerial imagery gets classified for urban planning. It was slow, occasionally tedious, and the first time I actually enjoyed reading academic papers instead of just surviving them.

Before that I spent a year designing graphics for AIESEC in Kathmandu. Nothing in a classroom has taught me as much about actually finishing things.

I don't have a tidy narrative yet about where this is going — somewhere between hardware, research, and whatever problem grabs me next. I like things I can build and take apart. I'm trying to read more, build more, and get comfortable saying "I don't know yet" about the parts I actually don't know yet.

If you're working on something in electronics or research and think I'd be useful, I'd like to hear about it.
